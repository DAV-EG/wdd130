Nigeria

Nigeria is a populous, diverse country in West Africa with a strong economy driven by oil, agriculture, and services. Its key cities, Lagos and Abuja, are major cultural and economic centers.Nigeria, located in West Africa, is Africa's most populous country with over 223 million people and a rich cultural diversity, home to over 250 ethnic groups and 500 languages. Its economy is heavily reliant on oil, though agriculture and services also play significant roles. Nigeria operates as a federal republic with a complex political system and has a history marked by colonial rule, military coups, and a transition to democracy in 1999. While the country is rich in natural resources, it faces challenges such as poverty, corruption, inadequate infrastructure, and environmental degradation. Despite these obstacles, Nigeria remains a key political and economic force in Africa, with major influence in regional and international organizations.
